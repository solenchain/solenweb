Solen Wallet Privacy Policy

This policy applies to the Solen Wallet browser extension and desktop application.

Summary

Solen Wallet is designed with privacy as a core principle. Your keys, accounts, and transaction data stay on your device. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal information to our servers.

Data We Do Not Collect

  • We do not collect personal information, email addresses, or IP addresses
  • We do not track your browsing activity or websites you visit
  • We do not have access to your private keys, passwords, or seed phrases
  • We do not store any user data on external servers
  • We do not use analytics, tracking pixels, or third-party tracking services

Data Stored Locally on Your Device

The following data is stored locally in your browser's extension storage (chrome.storage) or on your device's filesystem for the desktop application:

  • Account information — account names, public keys, and encrypted private keys
  • Wallet password hash — a bcrypt hash of your password for local verification (your password is never stored in plain text)
  • Network preferences — your selected network (mainnet, testnet, devnet)
  • Connected sites — a list of websites you have approved to connect to your wallet
  • Lock timeout setting — your auto-lock preference

All sensitive data (private keys) is encrypted with your wallet password before being stored. The encryption key is derived from your password and never leaves your device.

Network Communication

The wallet communicates with the Solen blockchain network for the following purposes only:

  • Balance queries — fetching your account balance and token balances from the Solen RPC node
  • Transaction history — fetching recent transactions from the Solen explorer API
  • Transaction submission — submitting signed transactions to the Solen network when you approve a send or dApp interaction

These requests are made directly to public Solen blockchain infrastructure (RPC nodes and explorer APIs). No data passes through intermediary servers controlled by us.

dApp Interactions

When a website requests to connect to your wallet:

  • You are shown a clear approval prompt with the website's origin
  • No data is shared until you explicitly approve
  • You can disconnect any site at any time
  • Connected sites can only see your public account address — they cannot access your private keys or sign transactions without your explicit approval for each transaction

Permissions

The browser extension requests the following permissions:

  • storage — to save your encrypted wallet data locally
  • activeTab — to inject the wallet provider (window.solen) into web pages so dApps can interact with the wallet

Open Source

The Solen Wallet is open source. You can inspect the source code to verify these privacy claims at github.com/Solen-Blockchain.

Children's Privacy

Solen Wallet is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date.
